THE NOTES

Are the following statements sensible/correct? Briefly motivate your answer.

The mean is strongly affected by extreme values.
True. The mean is given by (x1 + . . . + xn)/n so it always changes with the change of any value xi.
In systematic sampling, we simply use results that are very easy to get.
False. Results that are very easy to get are used in convenience sampling.
A bar chart can be used to visualize data at the ratio level of measurement.
False. Bar chart can be used only for qualitative data, at the nominal or ordinal level of measurement.
Download speeds (in MB/s) are at the interval level of measurement.
False. Download speeds are at the ratio level of measurement, since there is a natural zero starting point.
According to the “68–95–99.7” rule, about 95% of the data points in a sample originating from a normal distribution will fall within 3 standard deviations of the population mean.
Incorrect. About 95% of the data points in a sample originating from a normal distribution will fall within 2 standard deviations of the population mean.
The sample mean and the sample mode are always equal for data at the ordinal level of measurement.
Incorrect. The sample mean cannot be computed for ordinal data.
The sample standard deviation cannot be computed if the sample size n equals 1.
Correct. In the formula for sample standard deviation we divide by n − 1, so for n = 1 the sample standard deviation cannot be computed.
The probability of a union of two events is always less than or equal to the sum of the probabilities of these two events.
Correct. P(A∪B) = P(A)+P(B)−P(A∩B) and P(A∩B) is nonnegative, so P(A∪B) ≤ P(A) + P(B).
